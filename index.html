MY-STORY

From late-night coding to building self-running businesses (and a little hemp magic)

This is a playful, honest run-through of how I learned to make machines think, joined Samsung to push AI further, then decided to build businesses that honor nature — especially hemp — so people can reclaim consciousness while tech handles the grind.

Part memoir, part manifesto — tech meets nature, with jokes sprinkled like semicolons.

Joke: I started debugging my life before I debugged code — stack traces are surprisingly helpful for relationships.

How I started programming — curious, caffeinated, night owl

Like many, it began with curiosity: what if the computer did the boring stuff for me? I was that kid who took apart toys and glued them back together. Then I found code — and the thrill of making something that didn’t exist a second before.

First language: too many. First real project: a tiny game that crashed gloriously but taught me exceptions exist for a reason. Debugging taught patience — and that you can always blame the compiler when in doubt.

  • Python
  • JavaScript
  • ML basics
  • Systems thinking

Landing at Samsung — yes, really. (Spoiler: persistence + problem-solving)

Challenge: I wanted to work where scale mattered — problems that touch millions.

I prepared like an athlete: projects, open-source contributions, and a few portfolio pieces that showed I could ship. Interviews were tough, but I treated them like experiments: hypothesis, test, iterate.

Outcome: I joined Samsung as an AI engineer — building systems that think, optimize and sometimes argue with humans (friendly arguments only).

Takeaway: Be boringly consistent. Build things. Ship them. Repeat.

Starting my first hemp company — business, purpose, and a stubborn love for plants

After experiencing the scale of tech, I asked: what if we applied automation to products that heal? Hemp felt like an obvious bridge — plant-based, resilient, and surrounded by stigma that needed honest storytelling.

I built a small team, focused on ethical sourcing, clear lab testing, and educational content. The goal was never to be loud — it was to be credible.

Joke: People asked if hemp made code compile faster. I said: only if you also give the compiler a coffee break.

Brand values: transparency, sustainability, education.

Workshops for Future Engineers — Building Minds that Build Solutions

I conduct workshops for final-year engineering students to help them work on hands-on AI projects. The goal is simple — to make the youth industry-ready, confident, and capable of using AI to solve real-world problems.

These workshops are not just about code — they’re about thinking like an engineer: identifying problems, designing systems, and creating opportunities that can uplift others. I remind every student that an engineer’s true purpose is to build bridges — not just of steel, but of opportunity.

Joke: My students say I debug their thinking patterns before their code. I say that’s version control for the mind.

Why hemp — and why break the stigma?

I chose hemp because it’s a plant that has been misunderstood. Technology replaced physical labour long ago; now AI is replacing bits of our mental labour. That creates a choice: either be replaced, or evolve. I choose evolution.

Hemp, to me, is more than a product — it’s a doorway to conversations about consciousness, resilience, and reconnecting with nature. It helps people slow down and ask different questions — the kind machines can't yet answer.

What AI can do for businesses — short & sharp

  • Automate routine: customer replies, inventory, supply-chain alerts.
  • Personalize at scale: product recommendations and educational content matched to user readiness.
  • Predict and optimize: demand forecasting, pricing A/B testing, and campaign ROI.

When combined, AI frees humans to focus on strategy, storytelling, and care — things machines can't genuinely feel (yet).

Mini case: We built a chatbot that handled 60% of basic customer queries, letting the team handle deep consults. Sales rose; burnout dropped.

Joke: Let the bots handle the small talk — humans keep the deep tea sessions.
